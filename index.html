Tech Spotlight: The Agent Infrastructure Revolution

Understanding the foundation being built for tomorrow's AI workforce

AI "agents" are like digital interns that can take actions for you—send emails, pull reports, book appointments—but right now they struggle because every app has its own unique "lock and key" (API) that makes it hard for agents to plug in or communicate with each other.

To fix this, tech companies are building new standards: MCP (a universal plug for tools), A2A/ANP (a shared language for agents to communicate), and work by groups like W3C, IETF, and OpenID (rules for identity and trust). These are moving fast—MCP is already rolling out—but the full "agent highway system" where everything works smoothly together probably won't arrive until around 2028.

Bottom line: Agents are powerful, but the infrastructure they need is still under construction.
